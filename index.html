Catchment logic

RGS and JHGS have separate catchments — they are not the same shape. I pulled both schools' real boundary polygons from Buckinghamshire Council's own GIS service (the two overlays are official, not hand-drawn). They overlap heavily across High Wycombe, Beaconsfield, Gerrards Cross and Denham/New Denham, but diverge further out: Marlow and Bourne End sit inside JHGS's catchment (Priority Area A and B respectively) but fall completely outside RGS's catchment — confirmed by testing both addresses directly on Bucks' checker, where RGS didn't appear in the results at all. Each school's area is itself split into Priority Area A (higher-ranked) and Priority Area B (lower-ranked, still eligible) — both are shown, A as the solid fill and B as the lighter/hatched fill.

Confirmed in all 3 catchments: Beaconsfield and Gerrards Cross are explicitly named in Beaconsfield High School's 11+ priority area and Priority Area A for both RGS and John Hampden Grammar School's 13+ entry.

New Denham — now directly confirmed, all 3 schools. I re-checked this on Buckinghamshire Council's own address-level tool ("Find my child a school place"), which — unlike the ArcGIS checker used earlier — covers Beaconsfield High as well as RGS/JHGS. Testing an exact New Denham address (15 Orchard Close, UB9 4BB) returned: within catchment for Beaconsfield High (7.39 mi), within catchment for RGS High Wycombe (12.22 mi, priority-area rules apply), and within catchment for John Hampden GS (12.5 mi, priority-area rules apply). This supersedes my earlier answer, which was based on manually tracing static PDF maps and could only verify RGS/JHGS, not Beaconsfield High, for this specific street. Denham village and Denham Green (closer in, same side of the line) are covered on the same basis.

Seer Green — not explicitly confirmed: it's an adjoining village sharing Beaconsfield's HP9 postcode, but none of the three catchment maps name it directly. Treat the two Seer Green listings as a maybe.

Priority A vs B for RGS/JHGS: the tool flags "priority area rules apply" but doesn't state A or B on this results page — that distinction is decided address-by-address and matters a lot under oversubscription, so check it per-property before relying on the 13+ backup.

Always re-verify your exact address on the official checker before signing a tenancy — catchments and priority areas can shift year to year.
